Ecological Footprint Website Design

Introduction

This is a school project working with the city of Vancouver to present current Vancouver’s ecological footprint by designing an online information website and an offline product. The goal of this project is to let citizens realize our city’s environmental situation, and what can they do to protect our planet.

Skills

Through the process of doing this project, my skills in searching and summarizing information improved a lot. Now I can read and understand a new article in a quick time and summarize the key point. I learned how to design visual representations of information, such as using images. Explored the relationship between code and information architecture during I made the website page. I had a better understanding of the information’s hierarchy as well.

Challenges & Changes

The challenge of this project is how to transfer the academic report provided by the city of Vancouver to brief sentences that everyone can understand. I read and understand the report, and search online first to summarize the information.
Another challenge is how to encourage people to get to the website to know the information about ecological footprint. So we designed a cup sleeve for people who use single-use cups and sleeves. The sleeve has our website’s link and code for winning a free prize to motivate people to look at the website.

Reflection

Overall, our offline design and online design work well because they have a strong connection with each other. The sentence wrote on the sleeve encourage people to use a reusable bottle and reduce the waste and protect the environment. The content on the website is easy to read and understand.
My team members and I worked well on collaborating and communicating. It was our first time to made a complete website, from designing layout and information to coding the website. If we have more time, we will work on HTML and CSS to make the website more organized and more readable on different size of screens.
