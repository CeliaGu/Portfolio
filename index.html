Virtual Reality Headset Design

Introduction

This is the final project of the material design course which is asked us to explore the possibilities of conceiving and constructing a Virtual Reality (VR) headset without electronic components. The materials that our group is assigned are metal and plastic which are easy to manufacture and not cost-prohibitive.

Skills

Researching and using different materials open up my abilities as a researcher and model maker. Before the course, I worked with mostly wood-based materials like paper as well as thin plastic and acrylic sheets. Now I think I can make higher fidelity prototypes/models. Additionally, having access to solid space in the school with all the tools and machinery allow me to be more creative and ambitious since I don’t need to worry about how to make the special shape or the cost of the machine.

Challenges & Changes

We face the precision issue when we transfer the initial sketch to the actual model. Because the plastic is laser-cut and we need to bend the plastic manually, the dimensions are hard to predict and calculate. The pieces end up not fitting very well, so we bend the plastic edges more and add TPE plastic to fill the gap.
Another challenge is to find out which glue is the most effective in bonding metal and plastic together. This is achieved by researching and testing different products to find the one that matched our needs.

Reflection

Our VR headset is designed for the interior designer for their clients to have a better understanding of the design. The VR headset is mainly composed of laser-cut acrylic plastic and hand-cut stainless steel. There is a magnet between the front surface and the body so the user can easily open the front cover to insert their phone.
Since my group members and I have worked together previously, we know who is more confident in doing a particular phase. Therefore we collaborate with each other well. We learn how to design a product from designing, material choosing, prototyping, final model making, and finishing. We will work on decorating the headset if we have more time.

Ecological Footprint Website Design

Introduction

This is a school project working with the city of Vancouver to present current Vancouver’s ecological footprint by designing an online information website and an offline product. The goal of this project is to let citizens realize our city’s environmental situation, and what can they do to protect our planet.

Skills

Through the process of doing this project, my skills in searching and summarizing information improved a lot. Now I can read and understand a new article in a quick time and summarize the key point. I learned how to design visual representations of information, such as using images. Explored the relationship between code and information architecture during I made the website page. I had a better understanding of the information’s hierarchy as well.

Challenges & Changes

The challenge of this project is how to transfer the academic report provided by the city of Vancouver to brief sentences that everyone can understand. I read and understand the report, and search online first to summarize the information.
Another challenge is how to encourage people to get to the website to know the information about ecological footprint. So we designed a cup sleeve for people who use single-use cups and sleeves. The sleeve has our website’s link and code for winning a free prize to motivate people to look at the website.

Reflection

Overall, our offline design and online design work well because they have a strong connection with each other. The sentence wrote on the sleeve encourage people to use a reusable bottle and reduce the waste and protect the environment. The content on the website is easy to read and understand.
My team members and I worked well on collaborating and communicating. It was our first time to made a complete website, from designing layout and information to coding the website. If we have more time, we will work on HTML and CSS to make the website more organized and more readable on different size of screens.
