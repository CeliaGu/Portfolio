Virtual Reality Headset Design

Introduction

This is the final project of the material design course which is asked us to explore the possibilities of conceiving and constructing a Virtual Reality (VR) headset without electronic components. The materials that our group is assigned are metal and plastic which are easy to manufacture and not cost-prohibitive.

Skills

Researching and using different materials open up my abilities as a researcher and model maker. Before the course, I worked with mostly wood-based materials like paper as well as thin plastic and acrylic sheets. Now I think I can make higher fidelity prototypes/models. Additionally, having access to solid space in the school with all the tools and machinery allow me to be more creative and ambitious since I donâ€™t need to worry about how to make the special shape or the cost of the machine.

Challenges & Changes

We face the precision issue when we transfer the initial sketch to the actual model. Because the plastic is laser-cut and we need to bend the plastic manually, the dimensions are hard to predict and calculate. The pieces end up not fitting very well, so we bend the plastic edges more and add TPE plastic to fill the gap.
Another challenge is to find out which glue is the most effective in bonding metal and plastic together. This is achieved by researching and testing different products to find the one that matched our needs.

Reflection

Our VR headset is designed for the interior designer for their clients to have a better understanding of the design. The VR headset is mainly composed of laser-cut acrylic plastic and hand-cut stainless steel. There is a magnet between the front surface and the body so the user can easily open the front cover to insert their phone.
Since my group members and I have worked together previously, we know who is more confident in doing a particular phase. Therefore we collaborate with each other well. We learn how to design a product from designing, material choosing , prototyping, final model making, and finishing. We will work on decorating the headset if we have more time.
